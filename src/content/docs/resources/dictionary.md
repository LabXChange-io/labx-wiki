### A Dictionary of Common Laboratory Space Terms

<div id="A"><b><u>- A -</u></b> <a href="#top">Back to top</a><br /></div><br />
Account Number - A unique identifier assigned to a patient or billing entity.<br /><br />
Advance Beneficiary Notice (ABN) - A notice given to Medicare beneficiaries to inform them that a service may not be covered.<br /><br />
Assignment of Benefits (AOB) - An agreement where a patient authorizes payment directly to a healthcare provider.<br /><br />

<div id="B"><b><u>- B -</u></b> <a href="#top">Back to top</a><br /></div><br />Balance Billing - The practice of billing a patient for the difference between the provider's charge and the allowed amount by insurance.
<br /><br />
Beneficiary - An individual who receives benefits from an insurance policy or government program.
<br /><br />
Billing Cycle - The time frame in which a provider submits claims and processes payments.
<br /><br />

<div id="C"><b><u>- C -</u></b> <a href="#top">Back to top</a><br /></div><br />
Capitation - A payment model where a provider is paid a set amount per patient regardless of services rendered.<br /><br />
Claim - A request for payment submitted to an insurance company for services provided.<br /><br />
Client Bill - A pay type that means billing the doctor's office so they can pass it to the patient themselves.<br /><br />
Coding - The process of translating medical procedures and diagnoses into standardized codes for billing.<br /><br />
Co-pay - A fixed amount a patient must pay for a healthcare service, as dictated by their insurance plan.<br /><br />

<div id="D"><b><u>- D -</u></b> <a href="#top">Back to top</a><br /></div><br />
Date of Service - The date a medical service was provided to a patient.<br /><br />
Deductible - The amount a patient must pay out-of-pocket before insurance coverage begins.<br /><br />
Diagnosis Code - A standardized code that represents a patient's medical condition.<br /><br />
Diagnosis-Related Group - A system of classifying hospital cases to determine reimbursement rates.<br /><br />

<div id="E"><b><u>- E -</u></b> <a href="#top">Back to top</a><br /></div><br />
Electronic Health Record - A digital version of a patient's medical history maintained by a provider.<br /><br />
Encounter - A documented interaction between a patient and a healthcare provider.<br /><br />
Explanation of Benefits - A statement from an insurance company detailing what services were covered and any patient responsibility.<br /><br />

<div id="F"><b><u>- F -</u></b> <a href="#top">Back to top</a><br /></div><br />
Fee Schedule - A list of charges for medical services established by a healthcare provider or insurance company.<br /><br />
Fiscal Intermediary - An entity that processes Medicare claims.<br /><br />
Formulary - A list of prescription medications covered by an insurance plan.<br /><br />

<div id="G"><b><u>- G -</u></b> <a href="#top">Back to top</a><br /></div><br />
Guarantor - The holder of an insurance policy.<br /><br />
Global Billing - A billing method where all services related to a procedure are bundled into a single charge.<br /><br />
Group Policy - An insurance plan covering multiple individuals, usually offered by employers.<br /><br />

<div id="H"><b><u>- H -</u></b> <a href="#top">Back to top</a><br /></div><br />
Healthcare Common Procedure Coding System - A coding system used for billing Medicare and Medicaid services.<br /><br />
Health Maintenance Organization - A type of health insurance that requires patients to use a specific network of providers.<br /><br />
HIPAA (Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act) - A law that protects patient privacy and health information.<br /><br />

<div id="I"><b><u>- I -</u></b> <a href="#top">Back to top</a><br /></div><br />
Insurance - A pay type meaning the patient's insurance is paying.<br /><br />
In-Network - Providers and facilities that have agreements with an insurance company for discounted rates.<br /><br />
International Classification of Diseases, 10th Edition (ICD-10) - A coding system for diagnoses and medical conditions.<br /><br />
Invoice - A document detailing services rendered and associated charges.<br /><br />

<div id="J"><b><u>- J -</u></b> <a href="#top">Back to top</a><br /></div><br />
J-Code - A code used for billing injectable medications under Medicare and insurance claims.<br /><br />
Justification Letter - A document submitted to an insurer explaining the necessity of a procedure or service.<br /><br />

<div id="K"><b><u>- K -</u></b> <a href="#top">Back to top</a><br /></div><br />
Kickback - An illegal payment made in exchange for patient referrals or services.<br /><br />
Karyotyping - A laboratory test that examines chromosomes for abnormalities.<br /><br />

<div id="L"><b><u>- L -</u></b> <a href="#top">Back to top</a><br /></div><br />
Laboratory Requisition - A form used to request laboratory tests for a patient.<br /><br />
Liquid Chromatography-Mass Spectrometry - A technique used in laboratory testing for analyzing complex mixtures.<br /><br />


<div id="M"><b><u>- M -</u></b> <a href="#top">Back to top</a><br /></div><br />
Medical Necessity - A determination of whether a service or procedure is necessary for a patient’s health.<br /><br />
Medicare - A federal health insurance program for individuals over 65 and certain disabled individuals.<br /><br />
Modifier - A code appended to a CPT code to provide additional information about a service rendered.<br /><br />

<div id="N"><b><u>- N -</u></b> <a href="#top">Back to top</a><br /></div><br />
National Provider Identifier - A unique identification number for healthcare providers.<br /><br />
Non-Covered Service - A medical procedure or test that is not reimbursed by an insurance plan.<br /><br />
Network Provider - A healthcare provider contracted with an insurance company.<br /><br />

<div id="O"><b><u>- O -</u></b> <a href="#top">Back to top</a><br /></div><br />
Out-of-Network - A provider or facility not contracted with an insurance plan, often resulting in higher patient costs.<br /><br />
Outpatient - A patient receiving medical care without being admitted to a hospital.<br /><br />

<div id="P"><b><u>- P -</u></b> <a href="#top">Back to top</a><br /></div><br />
Pharmacogenomics - The study of the role of the genome in drug response.<br /><br />
Phlebotomist - A person who draws samples to send to labs for tests.<br /><br />
Preauthorization - The process of obtaining approval from an insurance provider before a service is performed.<br /><br />

<div id="Q"><b><u>- Q -</u></b> <a href="#top">Back to top</a><br /></div><br />
Q-Code - A Medicare-specific temporary code used for billing.<br /><br />
Qualified Laboratory - A lab that meets regulatory requirements for testing.<br /><br />

<div id="R"><b><u>- R -</u></b> <a href="#top">Back to top</a><br /></div><br />
Reference Laboratory - A specialized lab that performs tests referred by other facilities.<br /><br />
Revenue Cycle Management - The financial process of managing claims, payments, and revenue generation in healthcare.<br /><br />
Reimbursement - Payment received from insurance companies for healthcare services rendered.<br /><br />

<div id="S"><b><u>- S -</u></b> <a href="#top">Back to top</a><br /></div><br />
Self Pay - A pay type meaning the patient is paying out of pocket.<br /><br />
Superbill - A detailed invoice provided to patients for reimbursement from insurance.<br /><br />
Specimen - A sample collected from a patient for laboratory testing.<br /><br />

<div id="T"><b><u>- T -</u></b> <a href="#top">Back to top</a><br /></div><br />
Taxonomy Code - A unique identifier used to classify healthcare providers.<br /><br />
Third-Party Payer - An entity (insurance company, Medicare, Medicaid) that pays healthcare claims on behalf of a patient.<br /><br />

<div id="U"><b><u>- U -</u></b> <a href="#top">Back to top</a><br /></div><br />
UB-04 - A standardized claim form used by hospitals to bill insurance providers.<br /><br />
Unbundling - The practice of billing multiple codes separately when they should be grouped under one code.<br /><br />

<div id="V"><b><u>- V -</u></b> <a href="#top">Back to top</a><br /></div><br />
Verification of Benefits (VOB) - The process of confirming a patient’s insurance coverage.<br /><br />
Venipuncture - The process of drawing blood from a vein.<br /><br />

<div id="W"><b><u>- W -</u></b> <a href="#top">Back to top</a><br /></div><br />
Write-Off - The portion of a medical bill that is not collected from the patient or insurance.<br /><br />
Waiver - A formal document that releases liability for a provider or insurance company.<br /><br />

<div id="X"><b><u>- X -</u></b> <a href="#top">Back to top</a><br /></div><br />
X-Ray CPT Codes - Billing codes associated with radiology services.<br /><br />
Xenobiotics - Substances that are foreign to the body, often analyzed in toxicology labs.<br /><br />

<div id="Y"><b><u>- Y -</u></b> <a href="#top">Back to top</a><br /></div><br />
Yearly Maximum - The cap on benefits an insurance plan will pay for services in a given year.<br /><br />
Yellow Tube - A type of blood collection tube used for certain lab tests.<br /><br />

<div id="Z"><b><u>- Z -</u></b> <a href="#top">Back to top</a><br /></div><br />
Z-Code - A coding system used for proprietary laboratory tests.<br /><br />
Zoonotic Disease - A disease that can be transmitted from animals to humans, relevant in epidemiology and lab testing.<br /><br />
